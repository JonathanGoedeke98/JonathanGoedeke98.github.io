About Me

MSc Economist Student and MSc Behavioural Scientist with a focus on distributional policy analysis, social preferences, and welfare measures

Biography

Currently pursuing an MSc in Economics at the University of Cologne with a specialization in Microeconometrics, I also hold an MSc in Behavioral Science from the London School of Economics and Political Science, where I focused on policy analysis and subjective well-being. My research interests center on distributional policy analysis, welfare measures, and social preferences, exploring how policies impact different population segments, with a strong emphasis on equity considerations and well-being.

I investigate how individuals and societies value resource distributions, develop welfare metrics with a particular focus on subjective well-being indicators, and apply causal inference methods to assess policy effectiveness.

Beyond research, I am dedicated to teaching. As a tutor and exam grader at the Institute of Econometrics and Statistics, I support undergraduate students in building a strong foundation in statistics and econometrics.

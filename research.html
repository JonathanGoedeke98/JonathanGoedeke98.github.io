Research Interests

Exploring the intersection of economics, policy analysis, and human welfare

Distributional Policy Analyses

My primary research focus is on distributional policy analysis, examining how economic and social policies affect different segments of the population. As an MSc student, I am particularly interested in developing methodologies that can capture heterogeneous policy effects across the distribution of outcomes, moving beyond average treatment effects to understand who benefits and who might be harmed by policy interventions.

This research area combines causal inference methods with distributional concerns, aiming to provide policymakers with more nuanced information about the equity implications of their decisions. My work in this area includes analyzing the distributional effects of COVID-19 lockdown policies on life satisfaction and exploring how cash transfers affect political support across different population segments.

Through my studies, I am developing the analytical skills needed to conduct rigorous distributional analyses, including advanced econometric methods, causal inference techniques, and welfare economics frameworks.

Distributional and Social Preferences

I investigate how individuals and societies value different distributions of resources and outcomes. This research examines questions such as: How much weight should be given to improvements in well-being for those who are worst off compared to those who are better off? How do people trade off efficiency and equity considerations in their social judgments?

As an MSc student, I am exploring how to construct prioritarian social welfare functions using causal evidence on life satisfaction, which allows for formally incorporating distributional concerns into welfare analysis. I am also interested in how social preferences vary across contexts and cultures, and how they can be elicited and measured.

Through my coursework and research projects, I am developing expertise in experimental methods for measuring social preferences, as well as theoretical frameworks for understanding distributive justice and welfare economics.

Welfare Measures

I explore different approaches to measuring individual and societal welfare, with a particular focus on subjective well-being measures such as life satisfaction. My research examines how these measures can be incorporated into policy evaluation frameworks, complementing traditional economic indicators.

As an MSc student, I am studying methodological considerations about the validity and reliability of different welfare measures, as well as applications to specific policy domains. I am particularly interested in how subjective well-being data can provide insights into the welfare effects of policies that might be missed by conventional economic analyses.

Through my studies at LSE and University of Cologne, I am developing expertise in survey methods, psychometrics, and welfare economics to advance this research area.

Health Economics

Within health economics, I focus on understanding health behaviors and healthcare delivery systems. As an MSc student, I am exploring topics such as the effect of opening hours liberalizations on health behaviors in Germany and investigating determinants of vegetarianism using concepts of social norms.

I am also interested in healthcare provider behavior, having worked on projects examining effort provision in medical processes and incentives in healthcare markets. This research combines experimental methods with economic theory to understand how institutional arrangements affect healthcare quality and efficiency.

Through my coursework and research assistantships, I am developing skills in health econometrics, policy evaluation, and behavioral economics applications in healthcare settings.

Research Methods

Causal Inference

Applying quasi-experimental methods and research designs to identify causal effects of policies and interventions.

Experimental Methods

Designing and implementing laboratory and field experiments to test behavioral hypotheses and measure preferences.

Survey Research

Developing and analyzing surveys to measure subjective well-being, preferences, and attitudes.

Econometric Analysis

Applying advanced statistical methods to analyze economic data and test theoretical predictions.

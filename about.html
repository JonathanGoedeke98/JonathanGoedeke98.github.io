About Me

Economist & Behavioral Scientist with a focus on distributional policy analysis and welfare measures

Biography

I am an economist and behavioral scientist specializing in distributional policy analysis, welfare measures, and social preferences. My research focuses on understanding how policies affect different segments of the population, with particular attention to equity considerations and subjective well-being.

Currently pursuing an MSc in Economics at the University of Cologne with a specialization in Microeconometrics and Economic Policy, I also hold an MSc in Behavioral Science from the London School of Economics and Political Science, where I focused on policy analysis and subjective wellbeing measures.

My academic journey is driven by a passion for understanding how economic policies impact human welfare across different population segments. I combine rigorous quantitative methods with insights from behavioral science to develop more nuanced approaches to policy evaluation.

In addition to my research, I am committed to teaching and mentoring the next generation of economists and social scientists. As a tutor and exam grader at the University of Cologne, I help undergraduate students develop strong foundations in statistics, econometrics, and economic theory.

Research Interests

Distributional Policy Analyses

Exploring how policies affect different segments of the population, with a focus on welfare distribution and equity considerations.

Distributional and Social Preferences

Investigating how individuals and societies value different distributions of resources and outcomes across populations.

Welfare Measures

Developing and analyzing metrics for measuring societal welfare, with particular attention to subjective well-being indicators.

Subjective Wellbeing Measures

Exploring how subjective wellbeing data can be used to inform policy decisions and evaluate interventions.

Causal Analysis

Applying causal inference methods to understand the impact of policies and interventions on various outcomes.

Health Economics

Studying economic aspects of healthcare systems, health behaviors, and health outcomes.

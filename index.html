About Theses

About Me

I hold an MSc in Behavioural Science from the London School of Economics and Political Science and am currently pursuing an MSc in Economics at the University of Cologne, where I also teach undergraduate seminars in Statistics and Econometrics. My research interests include distributional policy analysis, social preferences, and welfare measures. I analyzed the effects of COVID-19 policies on life satisfaction and examined how the liberalization of shopping opening hours influences nutrition behaviors in Germany. Additionally, I am experienced in programming and executing game-theoretical experiments in oTree using Python.

Research Interests

Distributional Policy Analyses

Exploring how policies affect different segments of the population, with a focus on welfare distribution and equity considerations.

Distributional and Social Preferences

Investigating how individuals and societies value different distributions of resources and outcomes across populations.

Welfare Measures

Developing and analyzing metrics for measuring societal welfare, with particular attention to subjective well-being indicators.

Academic Projects

Distribution Sensitive Policy Analysis: Constructing a Prioritarian SWF using Causal Evidence on Life Satisfaction

Master's thesis | London School of Economics and Political Science | Supervised by Dr. Christian Krekel

A comprehensive analysis of how causal evidence on life satisfaction can be integrated into prioritarian social welfare functions for more equitable policy analysis.

The Effect of Cash Transfers on Political Support

Essay | London School of Economics and Political Science | Grade: 83%

An examination of how cash transfer programs influence political support among recipients and the broader implications for policy design.
